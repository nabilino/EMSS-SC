Flexible Spending Individual Plan Coverage   Close  Print 
St. Luke's offers Flexible Spending Healthcare and Dependent Care Plan coverage for employees.

Flexible Spending is an annual benefit election. If you participated in Plan Year 2007/2008 and you wish to continue, you must elect again for Plan Year 2008/2009.

This is a “Use it or Lose it” plan. At the end of the Plan Year, if there is any money left in your FSA that you are not able claim for services completed within the Plan Year, you will lose the unspent money. You have 90 days after the end of the plan year to submit claims, provided the dates of service for those claims fall within the plan year.

Each account is completely separate: For example, you cannot use funds from the Dependent Care Assistance Account to pay for your children’s medical expenses.

Elections are irrevocable unless you submit the appropriate paperwork to the Benefits Department within 60 days of a qualified change in status. Please note: The change you make to your FSA election must be consistent with the qualifying event. For example, if your qualifying event is the birth of a new baby, you can only increase (not decrease) the amount of your annual Health Care Account election.

If you wish to elect direct deposit for your FSA election, and do not already have this information on file with Pinnacle Pension services, you must complete a direct deposit form, attach a voided check and send it into the Benefits Department. This form is located on the “home page” under “forms.” Your current election will continue unless Pinnacle Pension Services are contacted to change it.
