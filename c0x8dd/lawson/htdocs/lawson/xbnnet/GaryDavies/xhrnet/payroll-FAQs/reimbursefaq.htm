Employee Expense Reimbursements FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Q: Will reimbursements be taxed as income?
A: No, employee expense reimbursements will NOT be included in taxable income.
 
Q: How will reimbursements be listed on the check stub?
A:

Reimbursements will be listed as a separate line item on your check stub and will not be lumped into regular earnings.
 
Q: Will it be included in Gross or Net Pay totals?
A: Reimbursements will be included in the Gross and Net totals on the check stub, however they will NOT be included in the TAXABLE income amounts. Again, reimbursement amounts will be listed separately from earnings for hours worked.
 
Q: How do I submit reimbursement requests? Can they be submitted with timecards?
A: Please DO NOT submit reimbursements with your timecards. These are two separate and distinct processes. We will accept reimbursement requests sent via inter-office mail, fax at (208)381-6901 and/or email (with all necessary attachments) to payroll@slhs.org.
 
Q:

Where do I find the forms?
A:

Forms can be found on the Inside St. Luke’s intranet page under “Forms, Images, Tools” under the section titled “Forms”. Please use the appropriate form: Expense Statement, Mileage Record, or Check Requisition.

 
Q:

Does this include mileage reimbursements?
A:

Yes. Please use the “Mileage Record”.
 
Q: Does this include Physician reimbursements?
A:

Yes. All reimbursements to individuals who are current St. Luke’s employees, and have an employee number, will be paid through payroll in this new process.
 
Q: Do I need to know my employee number?
A:

Yes. Neglecting to include your employee number could potentially delay payment.
 
Q:

What if I have additional questions that have not been addressed?
A:

Please call the general Payroll line at (208)381-2528 or email payroll@slhs.org.
