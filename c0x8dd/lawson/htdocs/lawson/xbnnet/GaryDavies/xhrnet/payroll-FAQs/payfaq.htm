Online Pay FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Q: When will I be able to see my pay stub online?
A: Since pay day is always on a Friday, you will be able to see your pay stub online starting at 12:00am Friday morning.
 
Q: If I'm not on direct deposit, can I still view my pay stubs online?
A: Yes.
 
Q: How do I print my paystub?
A: Most of the screens including the All Payments, Payment Detail, and Year to Date History pages have a Print button. This will print just the content frame, not the rest of the webpage. The actual Pay Stub that pops up in a new window can be printed nicely by selecting File | Print in the popped up window.
 
Q: I have direct deposit but I want to receive a paper paystub. What do I do?
A: It is mandatory that if you have direct deposit, you do not receive a paper paystub. This policy saves St. Luke's $69,000.00 per year. Remember, you can always print an online paystub too. If this is still an issue, we would like to hear why you want to receive a paper paystub, because it might allow us to understand an issue with our system, so please feel free to contact Payroll by finding their number on the Contacts link under the myHR Payroll section on the left side of the Portal.
 
Q: I do not want a paper paystub any more, so I need to set up direct deposit. How do I set that up?
A: Please fill out a direct deposit form: (Magic Valley or non Magic Valley) and if in Magic Valley, send it to Payroll, if not in Magic Valley, send to HR. All you will need is a VOID check from the account you want to deposit into.
 
Q: What is the difference between Gross Pay and Net Pay?
A: Gross Pay is what you earn before taxes, social security, deductions for insurance and retirement and other misc deductions are paid for. After all of these taxes and deductions are taken out, what is left is your Net Pay. That is the amount that will reach your bank account(s).
 
Q: Can I direct deposit to more than one account?
A: Yes. This is a very common thing, all you have to do is fill out another direct deposit form: (Magic Valley or non Magic Valley), explain on the form how you want your pay distributed, either by an exact amount, or by a percentage of your overall pay. Once you have filled out the form, if in Magic Valley, send it to Payroll, if not in Magic Valley, send to HR.
 
Q: How far back can I view my paystubs?
A:

Initially, the Treasure Valley and Wood River Valley will be able to see back a max of 7 years.

Magic Valley will be able to see back to January 1, 2008.

There are some system changes planned for Winter 2009 that will reduce the number of old paychecks Treasure Valley will see.

However, you will always be able to see all paycheck detail for the current calendar year, no matter what system changes happen.
 
Q: Where do I find my tax allowances and how do I change them?
A:

You can see your tax allowances by clicking on the Tax Withholding link under the myHR Payroll section.

This only allows you to view, if you would like to change, please fill out a W4 Form and return to the payroll department.
 
Q: Why does the Total Deductions on your paper paystub differ from the Total Deductions online?
A:

This is because a couple of non-cash deductions included on the paper paystubs and were grouped into the Total Deductions category.

Examples of these are the Excess Group Term Life (shows up as Ex Life under Wages on paper paystub), and the ING Retirement Company Match (shows as ING Mat under Taxes/Deductions on paper paystub).

For Excess Group Term Life: We're required by the Federal government to tax an employee who makes over $50,000 annually based on age, life expectancy, etc. The Federal government provides a formula by which to accomplish this task. Each payroll a calculated amount is added to the employee's wages, taxed, and then taken out as a deduction. The result is the tax paid on the excess group term life. In most cases, this amount is very minimal. Since the excess group term life is a non-cash earning, it is listed in the earning section but not the deduction section. It is, unfortunately included in the total of all deductions.

For ING 403(b) Retirement Company Match: This is the amount contributed by St. Luke's towards your retirement, which is a company match based on your current retirement elections. This contribution goes directly into your ING Retirement account from St. Luke's, and is in addition to the ING 403(b) retirement amount you have elected to put into your 403(b) account from your pay check. Since we list this on the paper paystub to let you know that we are contributing it at no cost to you, we list it as a deduction. Then it unfortunately gets grouped into the Total Deduction category even though its not a deduction out of your paycheck, it comes directly from St. Luke's separate from your salary.
 
Q: What is a company deduction?
A: A company deduction is some dollar amount contributed by St. Luke's as a consequence of you being enrolled in a benefit or retirement plan. For example, even though you might pay every pay period for Dental Insurance, St. Luke's also pays a certain amount to help you get your Dental coverage. This is to allow your benefit costs to be lower. You may also notice that St. Luke's will contribute a certain dollar amount to your 403b plan through a Company Match. This money goes directly into your Retirement account in addition to your retirement contributions from your pay check.
