myHR (ESS) Time Entry FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Q: What if I do not put in a pay code when I enter my time?
A: Error, Pay Code is required.
 
Q: What information do I need to enter if I only have one position?
A:  Pay Code and Hours
 
Q: How do I know which Pay Codes to use?
A:  In the Pay Code field there is a drop down listing all of your available Pay Codes.
 
Q: How do I enter PTO hours?
A: Select PTO Pay Code when entering your time.
 
Q: Can I enter Time in advance?
A:  Yes, but only for two pay periods in advance.
 
Q: How do I know if I have multiple entries for a given date?
A: Look at the “picture of a piece of paper” to left of your time entry for a given date and see if it has a split down the center or you can go into the daily time entry and see if you have entered multiple entries.
 
Q: What information do I need if I float and/or work multiple positions?
A:

1. If you float in your First Position to a different department – Pay Code, Hours, Process Level and Department

2. If you work your Second Position in the same department- Pay Code, Hours and Second Position

3. If you are working your second Position and float to a different department – Pay Code, Hours, Process Level, Department and Second Position
 
Q: How do I know if I have entered a comment?
A:

Look at the Clip Board to the left of your time entry and see if it has scribbling/notes on it.
 
Q: Can I enter my time from home?
A:

Yes, you can by accessing myHR via Internet Explorer and type in https://stlukesonline.org/myHR in the address field.
 
Q: Can I see my PTO & ESL Balances?
A:

 Yes, you can by clicking Leave Balances at the top right of screen.
 
Q: How will my manager know I have entered my time?
A: The manager will be able to view all time cards regardless if you have entered or submitted your time.
 
Q: When I submit my time card at the end of the pay period where does it go?
A: Time cards will always be available for your managers on their approval screen.
 
Q: What if I submit my time card and then need to adjust it and submit again?
A: If your manager has not approved the time card, you may edit the time card and resubmit. Otherwise, you will need to contact your manager in order to make the change.
 
Q: What if I enter my time card but my manager is not available to approve it?
A: Your manager should have a proxy identified in his absence. If not, Payroll will address the unapproved time cards and get verbal approval during payroll week.
 
Q: What if my manager does not approve my time card?
A: Your manager should notify you with any issue or concerns they might have. Otherwise, Payroll will address the unapproved time cards during payroll week and get verbal approval.
 
Q: If I need overtime approved by my manager how do I get my time approved?
A: Continue to use your department’s process and policies. Then your manager will be able to view and approve your time accordingly.
 
Q: What if I exceed 8 hours in a day? What do I enter?
A:  All hours are entered as REG hours until you hit 40 in a week or 80 in a pay period. Work weeks are considered Sunday through Saturday.
 
Q: How will my manager know I have entered my PTO?
A: Your manager will be able to look at all detail entered in your time card.
 
Q: What if my manager wants a history of my time?
A: Your manager will have access to two prior pay periods after payroll. If they would like additional time information your manager should contact Payroll Department.
 
Q: What if I no longer report to a department using ESS time Entry?
A: Go to Insidestlukes.org, Human Resources, Boise Meridian, scroll down to Payroll and print out a time card until your transfer is complete.
